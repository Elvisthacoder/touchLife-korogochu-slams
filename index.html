TOUCHLIFE CHILDREN CENTRE

It has its genesis in one Catherine Njumba who was inspired to start a daycare center in Korogocho slums, Nairobi in 2008. Earlier on a tragic incident had happened when 4 children succumbed to a fire when they were left home alone. Catherine was approached by several mothers, requesting her to care for their children when they go to work. Touchlife is a primary school and has a vision to become a childrenâ€™s home that will offer basic needs to these vulnerable children. Most of these children are orphans, single-parented more so amongst them are HIV positive. The center has been growing at a slow pace and managed to reach standard 5 this year, hoping to reach level 8. It has a current population of 74 children ranging between 4-12 years.

Vision

Touchlife aims to contribute effectively to the development of financial freedom and empowerment to the members and society.

Personal Stories

Jane

By Vincent   

A widow has 3 children who are of school going age. She does laundry for families and sells tins as a source of income. Her daily wage comes to a minimum of 50 shillings which caters for house rent, food, education for her children and clothing. Many times she is not able to sustain all this needs. Touchlife center has admitted these children and provides them with education and daytime meals for free.

Mary

By Tzviatko   

A 10 year old girl who lives with her stepmother as her biological parents died. Her stepmother overworks her by sending her to the market when she should be going to school. In addition to that, she mistreats her and denies her basic needs. As a result of this she went into depression which affected her school performance. Mary joined touchlife where she received education, food and a loving family. As a result became a stable happy girl.
